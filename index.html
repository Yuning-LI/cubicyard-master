The Ultimate Guide to Calculating Cubic Yards

Whether you are a professional contractor pouring a concrete slab or a homeowner planning a mulch garden bed, getting your material estimates right is critical. Ordering too little leads to project delays, while ordering too much wastes money.

This guide will walk you through the math, conversion formulas (like square feet to cubic yards), and coverage rates for common materials like concrete, dirt, gravel, and sand.

How to Calculate Cubic Yards (The Formula)

A cubic yard is a unit of volume. It represents a cube that is 1 yard long, 1 yard wide, and 1 yard deep. Since 1 yard equals 3 feet, a single cubic yard contains 27 cubic feet (3 x 3 x 3 = 27).

The standard formula for rectangular projects is:

Length (ft) × Width (ft) × Depth (ft) ÷ 27 = Cubic Yards

Example: You have a driveway that is 100 feet long, 10 feet wide, and 4 inches deep.

  1. First, convert the depth to feet: 4 inches ÷ 12 = 0.33 feet.
  2. Multiply: 100 × 10 × 0.33 = 330 cubic feet.
  3. Divide by 27: 330 ÷ 27 = 12.22 cubic yards.

How to Calculate Cylinder Volume (Columns)

Calculating the volume of a round hole or column requires a different formula than a rectangle.
Formula: π × r² × Depth
Where r is the radius (half of the diameter). Our calculator handles this math for you instantly.

Converting Square Feet to Cubic Yards

One of the most common questions we get is: "I know my square footage, how many yards do I need?"

If you already know the area (Square Footage), the math is even easier. You just need to know how thick (deep) you want your material to be.

  • Step 1: Determine your depth in feet (e.g., 3 inches = 0.25 ft).
  • Step 2: Multiply your Square Footage by the Depth.
  • Step 3: Divide by 27.

Estimating Concrete: Bags vs. Yards

For small projects like setting fence posts or a small patio, you'll likely use pre-mixed bags of concrete (like Quikrete). For larger driveways, you'll order a ready-mix truck by the cubic yard.

Use this chart to determine how many bags of pre-mixed concrete equal 1 cubic yard:

Bag Size (lb) Bags per Cubic Yard Yield per Bag (cubic feet)
80 lb bag 45 bags 0.60 cu. ft.
60 lb bag 60 bags 0.45 cu. ft.
40 lb bag 90 bags 0.30 cu. ft.

Visualizing Volume: Wheelbarrows & Dump Trucks

It can be hard to visualize just how much material a "yard" is. Here are some helpful comparisons for planning your logistics:

  • Wheelbarrows: A standard contractor's wheelbarrow holds about 2 to 3 cubic feet. You will need roughly 9 to 14 wheelbarrow loads to move 1 cubic yard of dirt or gravel.
  • Pickup Truck: A standard full-size pickup truck bed can hold about 2 to 2.5 cubic yards of mulch (which is light), but only about 1 cubic yard of heavy rock or wet dirt before hitting weight limits.
  • Dump Truck: A standard commercial dump truck typically hauls 10 to 14 cubic yards of material per load.

Material Density Guide (Weight)

If you are hauling material in a pickup truck, weight is your limiting factor, not just volume.

  • Concrete: ~4,050 lbs per cubic yard (Very Heavy)
  • Gravel: ~2,700 lbs per cubic yard
  • Topsoil: ~2,000 lbs per cubic yard (Dry)
  • Mulch: ~800 lbs per cubic yard (Light)

Materials Guide: Dirt, Mulch, and Gravel

Mulch Coverage

Mulch is typically applied at a depth of 2 to 3 inches to prevent weed growth and retain moisture.

  • 1 Cubic Yard covers: 162 sq. ft. at 2 inches deep.
  • 1 Cubic Yard covers: 108 sq. ft. at 3 inches deep.

Gravel & Stone

Gravel is denser than mulch. When calculating for a driveway, it is crucial to account for compaction. We recommend ordering 10% to 15% extra gravel to account for the material settling into the sub-base over time.

Topsoil & Fill Dirt

Like gravel, "fluffed" soil from a truck will settle once spread and watered. For large landscaping projects involving fill dirt, assume a compaction factor of about 20%. If your hole is 10 cubic yards, order 12 cubic yards of dirt.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate cubic yards manually?
To calculate cubic yards, you first need to convert all your measurements (length, width, and depth) into feet. Multiply them together to get the total cubic feet, and then divide that number by 27.
Formula: (L × W × H) ÷ 27
How many bags of concrete make a cubic yard?
It depends on the size of the bag. Generally, you need:
  • 45 bags of 80lb concrete mix
  • 60 bags of 60lb concrete mix
  • 90 bags of 40lb concrete mix
Always order 5-10% extra for spillage or uneven ground.
How much area does 1 cubic yard of mulch cover?
Coverage depends on depth. One cubic yard covers:
  • 324 sq. ft. at 1 inch deep
  • 162 sq. ft. at 2 inches deep
  • 108 sq. ft. at 3 inches deep (Recommended for weed control)
